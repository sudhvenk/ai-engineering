# Movies & Film Activities

Film-based activities provide entertainment, cultural exposure, and opportunities for discussion and critical thinking.

## MOVIE SCREENINGS
Group viewing of films in a communal setting.  
**Intensity:** Low | **Session:** 90–120 min | **Frequency:** Occasional  
**Benefits:** Relaxation, shared experience  

## FILM CLUBS
Regular gatherings to watch and discuss films.  
**Intensity:** Low | **Session:** 90–120 min | **Frequency:** Weekly–Monthly  
**Benefits:** Social engagement, critical thinking  
**Ailments:** Social isolation  

## DOCUMENTARIES
Educational films exploring real-world topics.  
**Intensity:** Low | **Session:** 60–120 min | **Frequency:** Occasional  
**Benefits:** Knowledge, awareness  

## CLASSIC MOVIES
Screenings of historically significant films.  
**Intensity:** Low | **Session:** 90–120 min | **Frequency:** Occasional  
**Benefits:** Cultural literacy  

## FOREIGN FILMS
International cinema with subtitles.  
**Intensity:** Low | **Session:** 90–120 min | **Frequency:** Occasional  
**Benefits:** Cultural exposure, cognitive engagement  

## FAMILY MOVIES
Age-appropriate films for all generations.  
**Intensity:** Low | **Session:** 90 min | **Frequency:** Occasional  
**Benefits:** Family bonding  

## MOVIE DISCUSSIONS
Facilitated conversations analyzing films.  
**Intensity:** Low | **Session:** 45–60 min | **Frequency:** Occasional  
**Benefits:** Communication, critical thinking  

## FILMMAKING BASICS
Introductory classes on filmmaking concepts.  
**Intensity:** Low–Moderate | **Session:** 90–120 min | **Frequency:** Project-based  
**Benefits:** Creativity, technical skills
