# PAGE 1 — CENTER OVERVIEW
## Concord Public Library (East Boston Branch)
**Type:** Library  
**Location:** East Boston, Massachusetts  

Concord Public Library – East Boston Branch is a neighborhood-focused public library that supports arts education, food literacy, and creative expression. The library serves adults, families, seniors, and teens through small-group workshops and hands-on learning experiences.

Facilities include a community meeting room, art workshop space, demonstration kitchen, and accessible seating areas. Programs emphasize inclusivity, creativity, and lifelong learning.

---

# PAGE 2 — PROGRAM OVERVIEW
The East Boston branch emphasizes **visual arts and culinary education**, offering beginner-friendly instruction in drawing, illustration, and practical cooking skills. Programs are designed to be low-intensity, interactive, and welcoming to participants with varying experience levels.

Primary activity categories include:
- Drawing, sketching, and illustration workshops
- Cooking classes focused on healthy habits, meal preparation, and plant-based cuisine

Programs are suitable for adults, families, and motivated teens, with many sessions scheduled during evenings and weekends.

# PAGE 5 — COMMUNITY & LOGISTICS
**Registration:**  
Programs are community-funded and require advance registration due to limited seating. Walk-in participation may be available if space permits.

**Accessibility:**  
The facility is wheelchair accessible and offers seating accommodations. Cooking programs can adapt recipes for common dietary needs with advance notice.

**Typical Attendance:**  
Most workshops host between 10 and 16 participants to support hands-on instruction and discussion.

**Seasonal Scheduling:**  
Classes are offered in winter, spring, and fall sessions, with rotating topics based on community interest.
