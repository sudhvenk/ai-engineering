# PAGE 1 — CENTER OVERVIEW
## Northwest Central Library
**Type:** Library  
**Location:** Northwest Boston, Massachusetts  

Northwest Central Library is a public community library serving neighborhoods across Northwest Boston. The library focuses on lifelong learning, creative expression, and practical skill-building through accessible arts and culinary programs.

Facilities include a large community room, art instruction space, demonstration kitchen, and small-group workshop areas. Programs are designed for adults, seniors, families, and motivated teens.

---

# PAGE 2 — PROGRAM OVERVIEW
Northwest Central Library emphasizes **visual arts and culinary education**, with beginner-friendly instruction and hands-on learning. Programs are designed to foster creativity, confidence, and community connection.

Primary activity categories include:
- Drawing, illustration, and sketching workshops
- Cooking classes covering fundamentals, global cuisines, and healthy habits

Programs are low-intensity, instructional, and discussion-based, making them accessible to participants of all experience levels.

# PAGE 5 — COMMUNITY & LOGISTICS
**Registration:**  
Advance registration is required for most programs due to limited seating. Waitlists are available for full classes.

**Accessibility:**  
The library is wheelchair accessible and offers seating accommodations. Cooking classes can accommodate common dietary needs with advance notice.

**Typical Attendance:**  
Most classes host between 10 and 16 participants to support interactive learning.

**Seasonal Scheduling:**  
Programs are offered in winter, spring, and fall sessions, with rotating topics each season.
